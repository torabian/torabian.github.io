My Grandfather, Karim

I started to know him when I was 15, 16 years old. Karim was an interesting person, really unique for the environment he was living on. His parents died, and world war 2, early when he was a child. Ever since, he was working. He was a tailor, started his business as a helper, in Tehran. Slowly building up some wealth, finally could buy a shop in Zanjan, married to a women 12 years younger than himself at the time.

Karim was incredibily calm. This is so different for me - I have never saw someone like that. He would always put all the pain, thinking into himself. Never heared his voice loud. Not even once. Truly he had to sacrifise himself, to grow and take care of 5 kids, and he did it successfully.

In his line of work, people came, he measured their body sizes, and then told them when they can come and collect their shirts. Some people, never came back. He even kept shirts for 5 years, in the hope that owner at some point will be coming to collect them. Never even allowed me to touch them, to keep it clean for all those years.

Initially I had some ideas to improve his shop and he was refusing. At the time I did not understand why. Now I hit 30 myself, I am getting a glimpse of the reality, that why he did not want to chance even an inch into things. Maybe that's how it's supposed to be.

I remember I was going time to time to his shop, and drink a tea there. He kinda was famous in the district, for the fact kept the same shop running for over 40 years. Every morning, he was putting some money for grand mother, for daily purchases.

This is not a secret for me how much gone sacrifised upon my immigration to Europe, but luckily I was there, when he was ready to put us behind. He was at work, at age 86 maybe even up to 2 or 3 days before passing away. Unbelivable for me. He was working on shop at age 86. And often I wanted to give up at age 25 even.

He got hard breathing in last days. Totally unaware of whats happening. We took him to hospital, but after a day doctor released him. Of course, doctors know who is gonna go and who is gonna stay, so we brought him home for the last day. In middle of night I heard my mom crying, then I knew yes this is it.

Karim doesn't have much left, written, or recorded at all. Maybe he will be forgotten after I die, but I wrote it here for myself to remember what has happened.
