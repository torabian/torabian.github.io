# What is waist of time?

As of age 32, I have complete different perspective. Waist of time is not doing the fun activity, which you truly want to do, and which is not compensating for an activity which you desire more.

I believe that for all people, regardless of they admit or not, fun activities, truly boils down with highest priority, having intimate relation with other people, spending with loved ones, and having hobbies they like, if earlier condition are satisfied.

A compensating activity, is a fun thing, we do, in case the real thing we want is hard to achive, or inaccessible. For example, if you are an overweight person, you might prefer playing video game, instead of going to a dance class or trying to attractive a partner. That's fun to do, but there were other things you truly desired otherwise. Another case might be you'd really rather
to visit your uncle, but since he is not available, you go out with a neighbour
that you'd prefer not spend time, if the more prefered person was available.

Life comes with harsh realities though, almost no one can spend their entire life, doing those. Loved ones leave us, at worst case by death, or finding a better partner, life circumstances change, chemistry dies and so on. And it's weird that every thing else is parishing, and people mindset change. By that I mean, in order to life get going, things have to be done, for poorer the harder - and for richer often easier.

Let's put it in simple practical examples. You want to spend all your life with parents, and friends in neighbourhood, but a job offer somewhere else seems to sovling a lot of future issues. Now you might like the job itself, but it's not what you truly desire. If you accept that, although you are going to get benefits, in fact it's a waist of time, because it brought a distance between you and things you truly desire.

On the contrary, on rare cases, someone might truly desire a job, as very satisfying fun activity, comparable to the top fun activities such as friendship and intimate relations. Though, need to be very careful, if the job is not compensating for not being good at them. For me personally very hard
to accept if someone wants to spend at work 14 hours if a loving partner
is waiting at home.

Based on the definition, you might find making a large income might be waist of time while you could enjoy a football match with your partner, then the question comes: Does a lot of incomplete learning, tasks, a waist of time?

Again we need to compare them. For example, keeping a bird might be truly waist of time if you are single and having hard time for relations, but if the life is already perfect in other areas, it can be a fun and not waist of time.

In modern days, waist of time usually refers to activities which do not make money, or tangible outcome. Learning Spanish per say, for a few lessons, might be that case. There is no way with a few lessons you learn anything, and all the effort is gone could be spent much better, just by watching a movie with a loved one.

The thing is, usually people think if they have to spend some amount of time waisting, even by progressing, then later there will be more time to compensate the lost time. For example, I told myself I'll be taking a job I did not like and suffered, to later have lesser debt to pay at the time. The real issue is, that time I worked under that stressful condition, has left a huge remark in my brain, which could be a part of fun memory if spent elsewhere.

One need to be extreamly careful how they prevent waist of time, if there are other priorities to be put on.

A stable foundation, for a healthy person, is a livable apartment, in a place they like, zero debt, possible income, legal stability, and physical attractiveness. In case a person is lacking these, unfortunately they need to waist some time, might go many years, to fix them. Having people depent on you,
such as a partner, and children even makes that more fragile and complicated.

Here we need to be very careful, with our plan. In order to build the foundation, it might be totally perfect to spend 3 years in Uber driving people 80 hours a week, because it would clean up the apartment debt versus learning Japanese, which has 1% become financially viable.

Although some people advertise against working class, that they are being abused for their time, this is far from truth. Assume you are a single person, randomly dropped at 18 years old, in a good health condition into an island. In the first minute, it becomes so obvious, that you can't enjoy the shade and coconuts and spectacle that much, rather have to solve shelter, water, food, and so on. After initial investment, there is a ongoing cost needs to go to keep things running, not to say to invest material for unexpected rain, events.

What I am trying to picture is, just being alive has a disadvantage in itself by design. Modern society could make this fact worse, or better depending on who you are. If you are good looking, inherited wealth - even a small apartment - that's a huge game changer, you'll live much better than the island guy. In contrary, if you are born with debt, bad childhood memories, being from an unsustainable country, and not really attractive, you'll be living much worse than the island, because not only you are far behind, add to that you are being judged and compared with those who are just by being them are better than you.

To make it simple, if you can't have the foundation, anything that you do, which doesn't bring you from a timely perspective to that foundation is a waist of time. If you working 90 hours a week on uber, makes to get a place for you in 2 years, but a great offer or startup much more in 4 years, you should stick to 2 years. It sounds unrealistic from what usually people say, of being smart. But who lacks foundation, needs to fight survival, and it's only defined in time perspective.

Another problem most people ignore is, as long as foundation is not there, any other activity is perceived by yourself and people around you irresponsible. Going to night club, when you are not attractive or overweight, is pointless. Going to ask for marriage, if you are not driving a good car, something to offer, a place to live, is pointless. Buying a painting to hang on the wall, if you lack the ownership of the place is absolutely pointless.

The bright side of this is, the faster you realise that, it would take you lesser time to fix it, if you are among most unfortunate, unplanned families out there. In fact, for our island guy, is much harder to build all materials needed for life, let alone to construct a comfortable house. Now with providing some service to society, you can get a lot of things. A skilled person can buy a dish washer fast, but if he had to build it from zero would take years.

Good news is, once the foundation is there in life, then any activitiy is not
a waist of time. Going to dance class, for someone who parks a Mercedes in
front of the class and has visible abs, is a perfect experience.
From this point in life, you want to focus on activities that they worth, waisting time for them. Here is when the art of finishing things comes and
is an interesting topic to discuss about.

Essentially it boils down to the fact that, we can obtain capability, to build,
explore, generate money, or tract attention on certian things, and most people
know they are better to have, but often hard. For example someone who can code,
spent a lot of time consistently building that skill, same goes for gym,
industrial design, doctorship. Basically all those things which are hard
and make money or attraction. On lower level, other skills, to maintain
things around you would be perfect to add up.

To summrize, make sure you are having what you want, and you are not compensating. If you are, why and for how long, and put metrics to end that.
